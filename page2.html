Linus Trovalds

1969 - present

The cretor of Linux

Linus Benedict Torvalds, born 28 December 1969, is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and, historically, the main developer of the Linux kernel, used by Linux distributions and other operating systems such as Android. He also created the distributed version control system Git and the scuba dive logging and planning software Subsurface.

The following list is a time line of Trovalds' Life

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1981 - His interest in computers began with a Commodore VIC-20, at the age of 11, initially programming in BASIC.
  • 1988-1996 - He attended the University of Helsinki, graduating with a master's degree in computer science from the NODES research group.
  • 1989- Joined the Fiinnish Navy Nyland Brigade in the summer of 1989, slecting the 11-month officer training program to fulfill the mandatory military service of Finland.
  • 1990 - He resumed his university studies, and was exposed to Unix for the first time, in the form of a DEC MicroVAX running ULTRIX.
  • 1991 - He wrote a Pac-Man clone named Cool Man. He purchased an Intel 80386-based clone of IBM PC before receiving his MINIX copy, which in turn enabled him to begin work on Linux.
  • 1997-2003 - Torvalds accepted a position at the company in California.
  • 1997-1999 - He was involved in 86open helping to choose the standard binary format for Linux and Unix.
  • 1999 - He was named by the MIT Technology Review TR100 as one of the world's top 100 innovators under age 35.
  • 2006 - approximately two percent of the Linux kernel was written by Torvalds himself.
  • 2010 - Torvalds became a United States citizen and registered to vote in the United States. He is unaffiliated with any U.S. political party, saying, "I have way too much personal pride to want to be associated with any of them, quite frankly.
  • 2011 - Subsurface was initially designed and developed by Linus Torvalds and Dirk Hohndel.
